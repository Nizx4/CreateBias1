Why Do Biases Exist?

Throughout evolution, our brains developed mental shortcuts—called heuristics—to help us make quick decisions and survive. These shortcuts allowed humans to react fast, even with limited information.

While heuristics can be useful, they sometimes lead us astray. When our shortcuts misfire, they create predictable errors in thinking, known as cognitive biases.

By understanding these biases, we can improve our critical thinking skills, make better decisions, and become more aware of how our minds work.
